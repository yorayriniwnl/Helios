Calm Under Pressure — Quick script

If a judge interrupts:

- Pause (0.8–1.2s), breathe, keep a calm face.
- Restate the question briefly to confirm: "Do you mean X?"
- Give a one-line headline answer (15–20s max): headline first, one supporting fact, then next step.
- If you need time, say: "Short answer: [headline]. I'll follow up with details after the demo." — then act.

Mental checklist (think these in order):
1. Who is asking and what decision matters? (align your answer to that.)
2. One-line conclusion (Yes / No / We will). Say it first.
3. One quick supporting fact or example.
4. A clear next action: demo slide, quick replay, or follow-up with logs.

Short, copyable responses:
- Direct yes: "Yes — we use role-based controls and signed evidence; that limits exposure. I can show the config after this." (headline, fact, next step)
- Direct no: "Not yet — we plan a hardened control in the next sprint; I'll share a timeline after the session." (headline, fact, next step)
- If unsure: "Good question — I don't have that exact number right now; I'll get the log and follow up within 24 hours." (own it + follow-up)

If cut off mid-sentence:
- Stop, breathe, smile briefly, and say: "Let me answer that clearly: [one-line answer]." Then continue with one supporting point.

Tone & delivery reminders:
- Keep your voice steady and slightly slower; pause after the headline so it lands.
- Maintain eye contact with the asker, then sweep the panel when you finish.
- Use the voice modulation guide and the recovery script if the demo breaks.

Practice: run Q&A drills with 1–2 colleagues; practice restating the question and giving the 15–20s headline+fact answer.
