Judge Reaction Anticipation — Predicted questions and sharp responses

Quick pattern: Restate the question → One-line headline answer → One supporting fact → Clear next step.

1) "How is this different from existing alert tools?"
- Headline: We attach prioritized remediation playbooks and evidence to alerts, not just notifications.
- Fact: Playbooks turn alerts into concrete, prioritized steps that teams can act on immediately.
- Next step: "I'll show a playbook in 30s, then share the audit trail."

2) "How do you avoid false positives?"
- Headline: We require corroborating evidence and surface confidence, not every noisy signal.
- Fact: Low‑confidence signals are grouped and deprioritized; high‑confidence alerts include multiple data points.
- Next step: "We can show an example where evidence reduced false alarms by focusing on correlated signals."

3) "How long to integrate?"
- Headline: A pilot is fast; full rollout depends on scope.
- Fact: We provide scripts and a silent demo to prove the flow with minimal changes to your stack.
- Next step: "We can run a short pilot and report back exact effort estimates."

4) "What if a remediation makes things worse?"
- Headline: Playbooks are safe-by-default and include dry‑run and rollback steps; risky actions require human approval.
- Fact: All actions are audited and reversible; teams control which steps can run automatically.
- Next step: "We'll show a safe playbook and the rollback steps next."

5) "How do you measure value / ROI?"
- Headline: Measure MTTR and incident impact reduction; we quantify value in a pilot.
- Fact: We provide clear before/after metrics from a pilot to demonstrate saved hours and reduced downtime.
- Next step: "Let's agree a brief pilot so we can show real numbers."

6) "Security and data privacy?"
- Headline: Minimal data collection, encryption, RBAC, and optional in‑customer deployment.
- Fact: Evidence is stored auditable and can be kept inside customer network or VPC.
- Next step: "I'll share our security brief and architecture notes after this session."

7) "Who owns the playbooks?"
- Headline: Teams own playbooks (SRE / platform) — we enable easy authoring and approvals.
- Fact: Playbooks are editable, reviewed, and tagged by ownership to avoid mistakes.
- Next step: "We can map ownership during the pilot and seed a safe example."

8) "Can we replay incidents or export evidence?"
- Headline: Yes — full timeline, logs, and signed evidence are attached to each alert for replay and audit.
- Fact: We offer recorded demos and silent playback for reproducibility.
- Next step: "I'll play the recorded incident or the silent demo now."

9) "How does it scale?"
- Headline: Designed to run with event streams and scale with customer load; we provide architecture guidance.
- Fact: We separate detection and orchestration to scale independently.
- Next step: "We can walk through a high‑level architecture diagram after this demo."

10) Pricing / commercial asks
- Headline: Value‑based pricing tied to outcomes; start with a pilot to quantify savings.
- Fact: Pilot results inform a tailored proposal focused on ROI.
- Next step: "We can prepare a short pilot proposal and pricing outline after the session."

Quick one‑line reply templates (copyable):
- Restate: "Do you mean [X]?"
- Headline: "Short answer: [headline]."
- Fact: "Quick fact: [one supporting fact]."
- Close: "Next step: [offer a demo/pilot/doc]."

If they press for numbers: "We'll run a short pilot and deliver exact MTTR and cost savings within the pilot window — I'll share the data afterward."

If interrupted: use the calm script (docs/calm_under_pressure.md): restate, give a 15–20s headline+fact answer, then offer follow-up.
