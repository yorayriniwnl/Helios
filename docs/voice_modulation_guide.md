Voice Modulation Guide — Sound Confident

Core moves: EMPHASIZE the one idea that matters, PAUSE so it lands, and CONTRAST to show importance.

Emphasis points (what to stress):
- Opening headline: stress the single unexpected word. Example: "Right now, something could be silently failing for your customers." [EMPHASIS: silently failing]
- Key metric or number: make the number slightly louder and slower. Example: "affecting 12,000 users" [EMPHASIS: 12,000 users]
- Impact statement: stress who/what is affected. Example: "This puts revenue and trust at risk." [EMPHASIS: revenue and trust]
- The single next step: make the action crisp. Example: "Step one: rollback the release." [EMPHASIS: rollback the release]

Pauses (where and how long):
- After any headline or claim: full pause 0.9–1.5s. (Let the room absorb the sentence.)
- Between clauses in a sentence: micro‑pause 0.3–0.6s. (Keeps rhythm, avoids rushing.)
- Before the ask/CTA: pause 1.0–1.8s; breathe and look at judges.

Annotated 10‑second opener (read with markers):
"Right now, something could be silently failing for your customers." [EMPHASIS: silently failing] (PAUSE 1s)
"We find it, explain what happened, and show exactly how to fix it." [EMPHASIS: exactly how to fix it] (PAUSE 1s)

Delivery tips to improve impact:
- Breathe before the headline (inhale 1s), then speak the line with steady pace.
- Slow your normal speaking rate by ~10–20% for clarity on stage.
- Use a slightly lower pitch for context, and raise pitch/volume 5–10% on the emphasized word.
- Smile briefly on transitions — it relaxes your voice and connects with judges.
- Avoid filler words. If you need time, use a silent pause rather than "um".

Quick practice drill (5 minutes):
1) Read the annotated opener aloud 10x, applying emphasis and the pauses.
2) Record one run, play it back, and note one place to slow or emphasize more.
3) Repeat until the opener feels natural and confident.

If the demo breaks: use the recovery line from docs/demo_failure_recovery.md, deliver it calmly, then execute the backup. Keep your voice steady and maintain eye contact.
