# Demo Timing Strategy — 3–5 Minute Presentation

Goal
- Tight, repeatable timing for a 3–5 minute pitch split into: Intro → Demo → Closing. Two ready variants included (3‑minute and 5‑minute). Use exact scripts and visual cues below.

Essentials (prep)
- Have a single preview slide with the killer hook and one key metric. Start timer when you click that slide.
- Demo tech: run the demo in a stable network environment; keep a 60s recorded fallback video and 3 screenshots (alert list, alert detail, mobile evidence) ready.
- Use a visible timer (phone or countdown overlay) and rehearse twice at full speed.

3‑Minute Variant (180 seconds)
- Intro — 30s
  - Hook (8s): "India loses over ₹30,000 crore every year to electricity theft and distribution losses." Pause 1s.
  - Problem & impact (12s): "City X feeders show 30% AT&C; theft drives most loss — outages and tariff pressure follow." 
  - One‑line solution & ask (10s): "Helios detects theft in streaming reads, prioritizes high‑ROI inspections — we seek a 5k pilot partner." 

- Demo — 120s (2m)
  1) Dashboard snapshot — 25s: show KPI map + prioritized alert queue; say: "We prioritize by expected INR recovery." (quick highlight). 
  2) Alert detail & explainability — 55s: open top alert, show time‑series + feature attribution, show expected recovered kWh/INR and ROI gating; say: "This alert is high‑confidence and yields ~₹X expected recovery." 
  3) Inspector flow (mobile) — 40s: assign alert, open mobile evidence capture (photo + geo + signature), show verification updating the dashboard. End demo with: "Alert → Evidence → Verified recovery." 

- Closing — 30s
  - Impact snapshot (15s): cite pilot conservative/optimistic numbers (e.g., recovered kWh and INR/year) from impact sheet.
  - Clear ask & next step (10s): "We want a pilot MoU and data access; we can start procurement in 4–8 weeks." 
  - Final line (5s): short contact/next step: "I'll follow up with the SOW and pilot plan." 

5‑Minute Variant (300 seconds)
- Intro — 45s
  - Hook (10s), Problem (15s), Solution (10s), Single metric + ask (10s).

- Demo — 180s (3m)
  1) Dashboard & priority logic — 40s: show map, filters (by expected INR), sorting, and how triage reduces noise. 
  2) Alert detail & explainability — 60s: deep dive into model rationales (feature weights, timeline) and ROI calc. 
  3) Mobile evidence & inspector workflow — 40s: photo + geo + signature + immediate dashboard verification. 
  4) Audit trail & outcome — 40s: show inspection report entry, pre/post KPI update. 

- Closing — 45s
  - Impact & economics (25s): short payback math and scale implication (100k meters example). 
  - Clear ask & timeline (15s): pilot partner, funding ask, and 8–12 week deployment start. 
  - Final line (5s): contact/next step.

Demo flow & slide mapping (quick cue sheet)
- Slide 1: Hook (start timer). Slide 2: Problem metrics. Slide 3: Solution summary. Slide 4: Live dashboard (demo start). Slide 5: Alert detail. Slide 6: Mobile evidence (video or live). Slide 7: Impact numbers. Slide 8: Ask & next steps.

Script snippets (cut‑and‑paste)
- Hook: "India loses over ₹30,000 crore every year to electricity theft and distribution losses — Helios finds it and turns it back into revenue."
- One‑line solution: "We detect theft in streaming reads, prioritize inspections by expected INR recovery, and close the loop with auditable evidence." 
- Closing ask: "We need one pilot DISCOM partner and ₹X to run a validated 5k‑meter pilot and deliver a full pilot report in 3–6 months."

Demo tactics & failure contingency
- Do a quick warmup: load demo pages 60s before go; clear notifications; disable auto‑updates that can steal focus.
- If live UI slows or fails: switch immediately to the 60s recorded video showing the same steps, then use screenshots to answer questions. Say: "We have a recorded 60‑second demo; live data will mirror these steps." 
- Keep the audience focused: narrate each click with intent — "Now I’m opening the high‑ROI alert — notice the feature scores on the right." 

Rehearsal checklist (3 runs)
1) Dry run without slides (timed). 2) Run with slides + demo (network). 3) Failure drill (switch to video + screenshots). Record timings and trim language to hit exact targets.

Timing cues (practical)
- Use the slide footer as a visual progress bar with minute marks (0:30, 1:30, 2:30, etc.).
- Assign a co‑presenter to watch the clock and cue you: "30s left", "10s left", and trigger the final slide.

Final note
- Keep the narrative tight: one hook, one number, one demo story, one ask. With strict timing, you convert curiosity into a clear pilot commitment.
