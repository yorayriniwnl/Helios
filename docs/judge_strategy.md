# Judge Strategy — How Judges Think & What Wins in 30 Seconds

What judges care about (practical)

- Clarity: a single sentence that states the problem, where it happens, and how big it is (people/INR/%). Judges must understand impact in one read.
- Impact: concrete, measurable outcomes — recovered revenue, AT&C reduction, outage reduction, and clear unit economics (INR recovered per inspection).
- Feasibility: credible timeline, pilot evidence, costs, team capability, and a realistic plan to scale without hand‑waving.

What judges will look for quickly

- Evidence: baseline data, pilot pre/post metrics, inspection logs or photos, and a reproducible demo or screenshots.
- Economics: simple payback math, per‑meter marginal cost, and expected ROI for the DISCOM.
- Execution: who will run pilots, what systems need access (MDMS, billing), and clear risk mitigations.

How to impress in the first 30 seconds (exact recipe)

1) One‑line problem statement (≤12 words): who, where, and magnitude.
   - Example: "City X feeders lose 30% AT&C — costing ~₹200M/year; theft drives most losses." 
2) One‑line solution: what you do and the concrete result.
   - Example: "Helios detects theft in streaming meter reads and directs inspections to recover kWh/INR." 
3) One hard metric (big number): pilot recovery or AT&C change.
   - Example: "Pilot (5k meters) recovered ~771k kWh/year → ~₹5.4M gross revenue in year one." 
4) One visual or artifact: a single screenshot (alert → inspection evidence) or 30‑second demo link. Visuals beat words.
5) Clear ask & next step: funding amount, pilot partner intro, or regulatory support — state exactly what you want now.
6) One‑line timeline: when the pilot starts and when judges will see results.

Sample 30‑second script

"In City X, targeted feeders show 30% AT&C and cost DISCOMs ~₹200M/year. Helios finds and localizes theft in streaming reads; our pilot (5k meters) recovered ~771k kWh (≈₹5.4M) in year one and cut AT&C by ~6 percentage points. We can run a verified 5k‑meter pilot in 8–12 weeks and demonstrate positive short‑term ROI within 6 months. Today we seek a pilot partner and ₹X to deploy — results and full playbook ready within one quarter." 

Quick dos & don'ts

- Do: lead with one number, attach evidence, and state a clear, small ask.
- Do: show the operational playbook — how alerts become inspected, recovered, and audited.
- Don't: bury the headline in technical detail or give vague scaling claims without costs/timeline.

Closing

Make the first slide or intro exactly the one‑line problem, one line solution, one number, one image, and one ask. Judges are short on time — give them a measurable story and an auditable artifact immediately.
